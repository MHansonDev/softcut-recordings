Sony Cassette-Corder CFS-240

Customized Antique Cassette Recorder

By far the strangest piece of gear in the collection. I had become interested in tape loops, and during my first visit to the local Goodwill I stumbled on this Sony Cassette Recorder from 1987. Much of the work getting it back to a functional state was spent using acetone and cue tips to clean off the decades worth of grime. Both the Aux input jack and tape belt needed to be replaced. Additionally, a motor speed controller and CV input were added to the top face to allow for further potential of the unit. While it may be the most unique instrument I have, it's also by far the most difficult and frustrating to use in a musical context. The noise floor is sometimes unbearable and the warble makes it near impossible to quantize to a grid. Though sometimes, it has a tendency to generate audible chocolate.
