Steinberg Cubase 10 Pro

"I think of Cubase as a Laboratory" - Hans Zimmer

I started my journey in recording using a free audio workstation called Tracktion 5 (now under the name of Waveform). I would recommend this workstation to any beginner, as I created over a hundred projects where I learned the basics of mixing, using VST plugins, automation, and so much more. However, as my projects increased in size and were supported by a cheap 10 year old Asus laptop, I found myself fighting the limitations of both Tracktion and my machine. Inspired by the minds of Misha Mansoor and David Maxim Micic , Cubase was an easy choice for a replacement DAW. Diving into Cubase 10 Pro took me to an unexplored environment where anything seemed possible. The world of sampling, EQ, compression, and the likes were all unleashed at once. Even several years later, I'm finding that the only limiting factor of this software is myself and the second hand desktop I build to handle the CPU load.
